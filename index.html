Diego Maradona

The Legendary Number 10

Diego Maradona lifting the 1986 World Cup trophy

Club Career

  • 1960 - Born in Buenos Aires Province, Argentina

  • 1969 - Recommended to Los Cebollitas (The Little Onions), the junior team of Buenos Aires's Argentinos Juniors

  • 1973/4 - Led Cebollitas to two Evita Tournament wins and 141 undefeated games in a row

  • 1976 - Made his professional debut for Argentinos Juniors as the youngest player in the history of the Argentine Primera División

  • 1981 - Made a US$4 million transfer to Boca Juniors and won the league title

  • 1982 - Transferred to Barcelona in Spain for a then world record fee of £5 million

  • 1983 - Won Copa Del Rey and the Spanish Super Cup

  • 1984 - Presented as a Napoli player in front of 75,000 fans with a signing for another world record fee, £6.9 million

  • 1987 - Won Napoli their first ever Seria A as captain

  • 1988 - Top scorer in Serie A with 15 goals

  • 1992 - Left Napoli after failing a drug test due to his growing cocaine addiction and signed for Sevilla

  • 1993 - Played for Newell's Old Boys

  • 1983 - Returned to Boca Juniors for a two-year stint

International Career

  • 1977 - Made his full international debut at age 16, against Hungary

  • 1979 - Played the 1979 FIFA World Youth Championship in Japan and emerged as the star of the tournament

  • 1982 - Played his first World Cup tournament in 1982 in Spain

  • 1986 - Captained the Argentine national team to victory in the World Cup in Mexico scoring "The Goal of the Century" and "the Hand of God"

  • 1990 - Captained Argentina again in the World Cup in Italy to yet another World Cup final

  • 1994 - Played his last international match after failing a drug test
